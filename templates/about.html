How this tool works

The Theorizr looks at what you've written and tries to guess which critical theorist is likeliest to have written it. It uses a bag-of-words approach to classifying texts, which means that it looks at what words you used, and with what frequency, to make a decision. It compares your word usage patterns to those of the critical theorists in the database.

The math behind this classification is a support-vector machine, which is quite complicated. You can read about it on Wikipedia, and it boils down to mapping the features of various texts in such a way that they are divided into clear groups (in this case, different authors). Then it assesses a new text (yours) and sees where on the map it lands.

The number of authors in the database is constrained by size and processing power limits.
